- **Task:** Where should we enforce API rate limiting — gateway or app layer?
- **Session:** 20260605-141200-rate-limiter
- **Started:** 2026-06-05 14:12
- **Chair:** staff-engineer
- **Seats (all seats speak in a meeting):** staff-engineer, security-engineer, qa-engineer, product-manager

---

## Round 1 — staff-engineer
Gateway-level (e.g. at the reverse proxy) is the cheap default: one place, no app
code, protects every service. App-layer only earns its keep when limits depend
on business state (per-plan quotas, per-endpoint cost). Propose: coarse IP/token
limits at the gateway now; revisit app-layer quotas when we ship paid plans.

## Round 1 — security-engineer
Agree gateway is the floor. But auth-sensitive endpoints (login, password reset)
need limits *keyed on identity*, which the gateway can't see pre-auth. Those few
routes need an app-layer limiter regardless. Don't let "gateway covers it" become
the reason login brute-force protection never ships.

## Round 1 — qa-engineer
Whatever we pick, it needs a deterministic test seam. Gateway limits are painful
to test in CI. If the security-critical limits live in the app, we can unit-test
the brute-force path. That's a point in favor of app-layer for the sensitive few.

## Round 1 — product-manager
Users hate opaque 429s. Requirement regardless of layer: a clear retry-after and
a documented limit per plan. Coarse gateway limits are invisible to users until
they hit them — fine for abuse, bad as a product surface.
