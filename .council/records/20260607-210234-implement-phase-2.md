- **Formats are the actual deliverable.** Highest-value output is making outputs

## Dissents (preserved)

**QA-engineer vs. product-manager — strictness of the conformance check.**

- **QA's position:** ship a full post-synthesis conformance checklist (~11 items)
  plus a bidirectional cross-link closure check. Rationale: formats only hold if
  something actively verifies them; documented-but-unenforced format drifts. QA
  specifically wanted the `Concluded` format checked for precision.
- **PM's dissent:** roughly half that checklist is "the chair grading its own
  formatting homework." Trim to two hard gates — *dissents-present* and
  *cross-link-closure* — demote the rest to advisory, and stop policing
  `Concluded` precision. PM's tripwire: an empty `Dissents` section on a session
  that obviously had disagreement. PM's metric: a human reads the record and
  states decision + next action without reopening the scratchpad.
- **Resolution (chair adjudication):** ruled **in PM's favor on scope**, but kept
  **QA's two load-bearing checks** because they guard things the templates can't
  self-enforce: (1) dissents-preserved-not-flattened, and (2) bidirectional
  cross-link closure. The ~11-checkbox list was replaced with those two numbered
  hard gates plus one advisory note. The `Concluded`-precision checkbox was
  removed from the gate — its format is still documented in the template, so
  QA's intent isn't lost, just demoted from gate to spec.

The live tension worth carrying forward: **QA optimizes against format drift, PM
optimizes against process overhead.** This session landed on PM's side. If
records later ship malformed in ways the templates don't catch, that's the signal
to revisit QA's stricter stance — it wasn't wrong, it was out-scoped.
